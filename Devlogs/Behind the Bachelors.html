Programming Cons

My least favorite part of of programming thus far is and most likely always will be Syntax errors and debugging. I find syntax to be annoying because the smallest type can break the entire game, then trying to debug the error afterwards is a nightmare.


Improvements

I feel I have improved since last week by doing today's coding task, as I have learned something as last week I had little to no knowledge of C# and Unity. I am excited to learn more about C# and Unity as the course progresses.


Errors of the day

Today, in my code. I made a slight typo which caused a big issue. This typo was fixed by taking a few minutes to read through the code thoroughly which had the typo and triple check it with the perfected code (code displayed in class). Conclusion

Overall, I didn't struggle besides with a few typos which were quickly snuffed out and remedied. I learned how to create a basic movement system into a game and I learned the basics of an if statement.

Welcome to "Behind the Bachelors"!

First Post Welcome

Hey everyone, and welcome to the first official journal entry for "Behind the Bachelors", this is a space where I will be sharing my educational journey throughout my bachelors degree in games programming. The course title is "BSc Games Programming and Development", this course is hosted by the SAE Institute.


About SAE Institute

The SAE Institute is a global leader in creative media education, offering a range of programs in fields such as audio, film, and games. With campuses in major cities around the world, SAE provides students with hands-on experience and industry-relevant skills.

They have 40 campuses world wide, providing their educational skills to 21 different countries. I personally highly recommend the SAE Institute to anyone looking to get into the creative media industry, as they provide a great learning enviroment and have a strong focus on practical skills and industry connections.


About the Course

It is going to last two years, with 3 trimesters each year, each trimester requires a submission of work.

The trimester plan is as follows;

  • "Project Planning & Theoretical Principles"
  • "Professional Development Scheme"
  • "Creative Media Major Project"

    • Each trimester will have a mix of practical and theoretical work, with a focus on developing skills in game development, programming, and project management. The course is designed to prepare students for careers in the games industry, with a strong emphasis on professional practice and industry standards.
